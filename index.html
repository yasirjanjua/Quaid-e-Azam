Quaid-e-Azam

A Politican, Lawyer and Founder of Pakistan

Muhammad Ali Jinnah

The Time Line of Quaid:

  • 1876 - Born to Jinnah bhai Poonja and Methibai Jinnah in Karachi.
  • 1892 - Weds Emi Bai and sails off to England for higher education.
  • 1893 - Joins Lincoln’s Inn to study Law.
  • 1896 - Enrolls as a barrister in Bombays' high court.
  • 1904 - Starts political career by attending Indian National Congress 20th Session in Bombay.
  • 1918 - Marries Ruttie at Jinnah House in Bombay.
  • 1919 - Criticizes British Government on partitioning of Turkey but doesn't agree on Gandhi’s non-cooperation movement.
  • 1927 - Presides a meeting of Muslim Leaders in Delhi and proposed some suggestions. These proposals are called “Delhi-Muslims Proposals"
  • 1929 - Presentes the famous 14 points to counter the Nehru Report.
  • 1931 - Takes residence in London for 4 years. He is disappointed by the attitude of the British and the Hindus at the round table conferences.
  • 1937 - All India Muslim League Session holds in Lucknow as Quaid-i-Azam begins reorganizing the party.
  • 1940 - Historic “Pakistan Resolution” passed at All India Muslim League’s Lahore Session. The resolution is also called ‘The Lahore Resolution.’
  • 1944 - Jinnah-Gandhi Talks holds in Bombay to discuss political solutions for Hindu-Muslim Unity. The negotiation fails to achieve its goal.
  • 1946 - Direct Action Day – Muslims protest against the British Government’s proposal of Interim Government that denied the principal of parity
  • 1947 - Pakistan came into being as a sovereign independent state for Muslim of sub-continent. Quaid-i-Azam M. Ali Jinnah takes oath as the first Governor General of Pakistan
  • 1948 - Dies at his home in Karachi the same day he is flown from to Ziarat (Wolpert Stanely, 1984: Jinnah of Pakistan). He is buried the next day.
